Monochromatic Sensitivity Breakdown

Traditional monochromatic analysis relies heavily on the sensor's ability to interpret luminous flux without the interpolation of a Bayer filter. In Noir cinematography, we specifically examine the Orthochromatic sensitivity curve which peaks in the blue-green spectrum, significantly darkening red tones and creating the high-contrast skeletal skin tones classic to the genre.

The Behavior of Shadows (Umbra/Penumbra)

Modeling the transition from light to shadow requires calculating the solid angle subtended by the light source. In a hard-light "Noir" setup, the penumbra width (p) is defined by the distance from the occluder to the surface (d) and the source diameter (s) over source distance (D).
